About Me

I am a Physicist working with Data Science, Analytics and Artificial Intelligence. I have a (mostly!) scientific background, having concluded the traditional academic flow of undergraduate studies, masters and doctorate degrees. Working in the field of Neuroscience has led me to learn a lot about several Data Analytics and Data Science tools and methodologies, including neural networks, support vector machines, linear discriminant analysis, k-means clustering and so on. I mainly researched methods to identify mental patterns in electroencephalography signals, which also enabled me to learn a fair bit about time series analysis, statistics and digital signal processing techniques.

I have worked both as a Data Scientist and a Data Analyst, focusing on data visualization tools, such as Tableau and PowerBI, and programming languages to query databases, such as SQL and PySpark (although I can also work with Python in general and MATLAB), developing analysis to bring business insights. I highly enjoy solving complex problems through modelling and extracting insights and patterns from data.
